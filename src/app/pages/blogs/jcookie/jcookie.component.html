Cookie

A cookie, in the context of computing and the internet, refers to a small piece of data stored on a user's device by a web browser. Cookies are designed to store information about the user's interactions with a website, allowing the website to recognize the user and remember certain preferences or actions.

They play a crucial role in enhancing user experience by enabling personalized content, authentication, and tracking user behavior. Cookies are commonly used for various purposes, including session management, storing user preferences, and tracking website analytics. It's important to note that there are different types of cookies, such as session cookies and persistent cookies, each serving specific functions

cookies are essentially stored as key-value pairs. The key is the name of the cookie, and the value is the information associated with that cookie. When you set a cookie, you specify both the name and the value
